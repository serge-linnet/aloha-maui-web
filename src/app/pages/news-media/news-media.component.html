On August 8th, 2023 wild fires decimated the Hawaiian Island of Maui burning an entire coastal community to the ground in minutes.

With 100km winds fuelled by Hurricane Dora 700 miles to the south of Hawaiian islands coupled with winds from a high pressure system to the North, the Maui fires spread rapidly & uncontrollably.

The residents got little or no warning. With fires encroaching from all sides, the entire coastal community was decimated within minutes. Petrol stations exploded. Cars, homes, shops, and wooden plantation-era buildings quickly burned to ashes. The harbour and its boats were completely engulfed in smoke and flames. The water was on fire from all the fuel.

Sirens intended to warn of impending natural disasters never sounded. Widespread power & cellular outages hampered other forms of alerting. No one saw it coming.

Retreating from the rapidly encroaching blaze surrounding them from all directions, and with no escape route possible, many residents tried escaping to the water. Many families and residents spent the night inhaling smoke & fumes in the water waiting for someone to rescue them. Horrifically many did not make it back out alive.

It’s a truly unimaginable situation with so many of the Lahaina community still lost among the wreckage & debris.

Drone footage shows destruction from Maui wildfires | ABC News

Drone footage shows the vast destruction in the town of Lahaina after deadly wildfires ravaged parts of the Hawaiian island of Maui.

The Maui wildfires are the deadliest U.S. wildfire in more than a century and with many still missing the Governor of Hawaii has warned that the death toll is likely to continue to rise.

The fires completely destroyed the city of Lahaina that had a population of about 13,000 and burned an area of about 3.4 sq. miles. In total it is estimated that the fires destroyed an area of approx.10 square miles across the island.

The causes of the fires remain undetermined. Climate change is believed by many scientists to be creating more extreme weather events. There had been warnings of an increasing risks of wildfires due to high winds and dry weather on the Hawaiian Islands.

Some scientists have suggested that the extensive spread of invasive species of highly flammable non-native grasses created large areas of highly flammable material. The spread of these grasses in areas that had been previously forested or used for agriculture has dramatically increased the risk and severity of fires on the Hawaiian islands.

As an island community heavily reliant on tourism (which accounts for more than 40% of the economy), locals throughout the island are directly impacted while relief efforts continue and tourists are asked not to come during this time. Many residents are now finding themselves without work while community relief efforts focus on helping those directly impacted by the Maui fires.

Local fire fighters & rescues teams were overwhelmed as they worked to fully contain the fires that were spreading rapidly in multiple locations across the island. Flare ups are still present one week on in local hot spots as firefighting crews continue their battle to contain them.

Large scale power outages have left much of the community without power and clean water which may take months to fully restore. With limited places to house people on island; many of those that survived the Maui fires are now in temporary accommodation or in the refuge shelters.


This is the biggest event of its kind in the history of the Hawaiian islands. The devastation from the Maui fires is heart-breaking to comprehend:

Many friends & families have lost their loved ones.

Over 115 people are dead and over 850 people are still missing.

Over 2200 buildings have been damaged or destroyed. The vast majority, some 86% of them, are residential homes.

Over 11,000 people have been displaced; tens of thousands of residents & tourists have had to evacuate the island.

1000s of families have lost their homes, with little or no insurance they have literally nothing left.

4 Schools were destroyed or damaged the day before the local children were due to return to school.

Many businesses were completely destroyed including surf schools, tour boat companies, guest houses, independent stores, shops and local businesses.

As a result, countless jobs are now gone leaving many locals without means or livelihoods.

Relief efforts that are very much reliant on outside support are being driven by local grass root community led non-profits and organisations.

The wider community on Maui is deeply feeling the strain as they rally together to support their Maui Ohana through the direct community led disaster relief efforts.
